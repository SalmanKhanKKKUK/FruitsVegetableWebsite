About Us

The kadai is a deep, circular, and flat-bottomed cooking pan made of various materials like cast iron, stainless steel, or copper. It typically has two looped handles on either side for easy handling. The word "karahi" or "kadahi" refers to the utensil itself as well as the dishes prepared in it.

Ginger, garlic, tomatoes, green chilli and coriander are key to the flavor of the dish. What distinguishes chicken karahi from other curries from the region is that traditionally, it is cooked without onions in the base, and instead uses just tomatoes, ginger and garlic. It is usually served with naan, roti or rice.
